Recommendations

Last updated September 9th, 2020.

Math recommendations can be found at Where to Start? This is a list of all other recommendations (more accurately, a list of things that interested me).

Books

Non-fiction

Den of Thieves - A fascinating account of the financial corruption committed by Milken and Boesky, and the ensuing legal battle between the prosecution and the traders.

Thinking, Fast and Slow - An interesting exploration of how our minds work, the distinction between the impulsive and more careful and rational side of our minds (System 1 and System 2), and an exploration of some biases and their causes. However, some of the examples are a little exaggerated, with Add-1 being egreigious - adding one digit to a four digit number under a rhythm is nowhere close to most people's mental peaks (any serious academic subject, gaming at a high level, and ostensibly, manuevering several tons of metal at speeds of 60 miles per hour should be much harder).

History's Greatest Hits, and Great Rivals In History - I am clumping these two books together

Fiction

Blindness - A harrowing tale of selfishness and surprising bouts of humanity arises when a country is hit with an epidemic of blindness. The writing style, however, is very difficult to parse, so it may not be for everyone.

Videos

Quantum Computing for Computer Scientists - A very accessible and fascinating talk on quantum computing from a mathematical perspective.

Don't Talk to the Police - A talk by Regent Law Professor James Duane on the various ways talking to the police can help the prosecution convict you, and why you should always plead the fifth.

Anime and Manga

Anime

Psycho-Pass - In a futuristic society, the criminal inclinations of everyone can be measured by a simple scanner. This leads to an extreme form of predictive policing, where the Public Safety Bureau enforce the law and treat people as criminals based on their criminal coefficient.

Death Note - Light Yagami, an idealistic and bored student, gets the Death Note, a book that can kill with just a name and a face. World-class detective L tries to catch Light, who has taken up the alias Kira, and an exciting cat and mouse game ensues.

Steins;Gate - The delusional Okabe Rintarou discovers that he has created a time machine by accident. Steins;Gate follows the consequences of him messing with time and the choices that he was to make. I also recommend Steins;Gate 0 after watching the original, though I offer no commentary on its content for spoiler reasons.

Manga

Yugami-kun Has No Friends - A unique tale about the voluntarily friendless Yugami, his weird idiosyncracies, and his interactions and developing "friendship" with the "normal" Chihiro Watanuki, who is unvoluntarily friendless in her new school.

Liar Game - The naive Nao Kanzaki enters a tournament of deceit, where each round consists of a game to trick their opponents out of money. Each round is psychologically thrilling, the games fascinating (and honestly fun, if the money element were removed), and the actions that the characters take are deeply revealing of the human psyche and nature.

Kaguya-Sama - Two honor students in love with each other are too proud to confess to each other, and a battle of wits and emotions ensues as they try to get each other to confess.

My Home Hero - An ordinary father's discovers that his daughter's boyfriend has abused his daughter, and kills him in fear for her daughter. The manga follows the tale of him and his wife disposing of the body and hiding the truth from the yakuza, and their daughter.
